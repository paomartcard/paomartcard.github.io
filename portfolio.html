PRODUCT

Moneypool Product Management Internship

This past summer I worked at Moneypool in Monterrey, Mexico. Moneypool provides users with the same functionality that Venmo provides for users in the United States.

During the first part of my internship I spent time developing specs and implementing a feature ranking system after noticing a need to be more data-driven.

The feature ranking system is basically a spreadsheet that used the ICE metric (Impact, Certainty, and Ease) to rank a list of potential features in order of importance. To use it you basically provide a feature and list what support you have for it (market data, estimates and plans, anecdotal evidence, ect). These indicators each have a weight depending on its importance and they output a final ICE score which is used to determine the feature’s ranking.

I noticed that proper feature prioritization couldn't really be achieved without having the proper metrics. It must be data-driven. Thus, I dove into the more analytical side of things and ended up using SQL and Mixpanel to create around 8 dashboard that measured AARRR and other vital metrics.

International Experience

Development Intern in India

During the summer of 2017, I worked for the Institute for Philanthropy and Humanitarian Development (IPHD), a non-profit in Jodhpur, India. social impact & leadership

Very Impactful People (VIP) Speaker Series

During my sophomore year I led an eight-person team in creating bi-annual, Stanford credited speaker series with over one-hundred students enrolled. We hosted 20 social entrepreneurs, all founders/CEOs, from organizations including B Corps, Kiva, ACLU, Path and many more.

The VIP Speaker Series is an effort undertaken by Stanford’s Social Entrepreneurial Students Association (SENSA). Although, this speaker series had been held for many years, under my leadership, we decided to innovate and create a new project in which we created five-minute interview videos of each of our speakers. We also designed the the way we did speaker outreach so that the speaker profiles would be better tailored to our audience’s preferences. This resulted in a 20% increase in number of audience members.

I thoroughly enjoyed leading this speaker series, because I love listening to the success stories of the social entrepreneurs and use them as inspiration for future social ventures.

Feel free to explore SENSA’s website to learn more.
