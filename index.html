About Me

As a lover of people and technology, I am interested in creating human-centered, tech-based innovations that foster positive social impact. Growing up in Mexico gave me exposure to the widespread inequalities and lack of opportunities that many people in developing countries face. It is my mission in life to use my passion for technology and human-centered design to find solutions for the most pressing problems in the developing world.

As a student, I have enjoyed working on a variety of projects that involve social entrepreneurship, tech, design thinking and international development. Feel free to explore them below and reach out to me if you have ideas for projects we could collaborate on.

Moneypool Product Management Internship

This past summer I worked at Moneypool in Monterrey, Mexico. Moneypool provides users with the same functionality that Venmo provides for users in the United States.

During the first part of my internship, I spent time developing specs and implementing a feature ranking system after noticing a need to be more data-driven.

The feature ranking system is basically a spreadsheet that uses the ICE metric (Impact, Certainty, and Ease) to rank a list of potential features in order of importance. To use it you basically provide a feature and list what support you have for it (market data, estimates and plans, anecdotal evidence, ect). These indicators each have a weight depending on its importance and they output a final ICE score which is used to determine the feature’s ranking.

I noticed that proper feature prioritization couldn't really be achieved without having the proper metrics. It must be data-driven. Thus, I dove into the more analytical side of things and ended up using SQL and Mixpanel to create around 8 dashboard that measured AARRR and other vital metrics.

Homemade Mobile App

Homemade Website

As part of my concentration in Human Computer Interaction within Computer Science, this past quarter I worked in a team of four to create a mobile app called Homemade. Homemade is the result of our quarter-long work in which we used agile methodologies and the design thinking process. We began with needfinding and ended up with a high fidelity prototype.

Through Homemade, travelers and locals meet, teach each other to cook a recipe from their respective cultures, and enjoy their meal together. As a result, the local learns about the traveler's culture, and the traveler has an authentic experience.

Learn more about Homemade by exploring its website!

IPHD India Development Intern

During the summer of 2017, I worked for the Institute for Philanthropy and Humanitarian Development (IPHD), a non-profit in Jodhpur, India. During my time there, I created a four-year strategic business plan that will impact over 400 beneficiaries and conducted field research to identify obstacles preventing girls from receiving education.

My time in Jodhpur was an eye-opening experience in which I learned to empathize with people that had completely different backgrounds than mind. The lessons I learned by living with a traditional Indian homestay family transcend any sort of formal education I have every received.

Very Impactful People (VIP) Speaker Series

During my sophomore year I led an eight-person team in creating bi-annual, Stanford credited speaker series with over one-hundred students enrolled. We hosted 20 social entrepreneurs, all founders/CEOs, from organizations including B Corps, Kiva, ACLU, Path and many more.

The VIP Speaker Series is an effort undertaken by Stanford’s Social Entrepreneurial Students Association (SENSA). Under my leadership, we decided to create a new project in which we created five-minute interview videos of each of our speakers and re-vamped our speaker selection process (resulted in a 20% increase in audience size).

I thoroughly enjoyed leading this speaker series, because I love listening to the success stories of the social entrepreneurs and use them as inspiration for future social ventures.

Library Paraiso del Libro

As president of the National Honor Society (NHS) my super-star team and I underwent a three-year process in which we fundraised (> 16K), built, and stocked a library in a rural elementary school near Monterrey, Mexico.

This is the project that stole my heart and has inspired me to lead a life of social impact. It involved lots of collaboration with the local community, and constantly overcoming set-backs. The library was inaugurated March 19, 2016 and named "Paraiso del Libro" by the students. To this day, students continue to enjoy the safe and creative learning space we were able to provide.
